Why vaping is a problem, not a trend

Even if “everyone is doing it”, your body doesn’t care about trends. It reacts to chemicals, nicotine, and addiction—no matter how cool the device looks.

Health

Your lungs aren’t a science experiment

Vaping can irritate and inflame your airways, making it harder to breathe during sport and everyday activities. Some people report coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath.

Breathing should feel easy, not heavy.
Brain

Nicotine rewires your focus

In young people, nicotine can affect brain development, especially areas linked to attention, learning, and mood. That “hit” can quickly turn into cravings that distract you from school and hobbies.

Short buzz, long distraction.
Money & freedom

Paying to feel trapped

Vapes cost money, and addiction costs freedom. The more your body expects nicotine, the harder it is to feel “normal” without it—and the more you spend just to feel okay.

Imagine that money going into your goals instead.
Vapes can contain nicotine even when not clearly labelled. Flavours don’t make chemicals safe for your lungs. Saying “no” once makes it easier to say it again.

Myths vs reality

A lot of what you hear about vaping comes from friends, social media, or advertising. Let’s separate what sounds good from what’s actually true.

Myth

“It’s just flavoured air.”

Vape aerosol can contain nicotine, ultrafine particles, flavouring chemicals, and metals from the device. It’s not the same as breathing in fresh air.

Reality: Your lungs notice the difference.
Myth

“I can stop whenever I want.”

Nicotine is addictive. The more you use it, the more your brain expects it. Quitting is possible, but it can be challenging—especially if you start young.

Reality: It’s easier to never start.
Myth

“Everyone vapes.”

It can feel that way, but many students choose not to vape because they care about sport, music, mental health, or just don’t want to be controlled by a device.

Reality: Choosing health is its own kind of confidence.
